Overview

University enrolment is an Observable tool with a series of visualizations that I designed and developed to explore post secondary enrolment patterns in Canada.

The main goal of this project was to transform a very large dataset (and some smaller related datasets) into insight; more specifically, help the audience discover the relationship between different dimensions.

Initial Problem

I will talk about a visualization in this project that I consider the most important:

International university enrolment by country of citizenship, gender, and field study, and year

I aimed to design a visualization that could find some patterns, and help find the right questions, questions like:

  • If some nationalities among international students are increasing faster over the years, are these values proportional to the population of those regions?
  • If female students are less likely to choose STEM, what are some possible reasons?
  • If female students are increasing in STEM enrolments, is it the same rate among different nationalities?

And then I could create other visualizations that try to answer those questions. But first there were challenges to overcome before I could achieve that; I needed to tackle too many dimensions (5 dimensions) in one visualization.

I explored different types of charts, such as the one below which is a draft I made in Tableau, but they were not helping with making the data more understandable.

Some problems with this draft:

  • difficult to compare the number of different nationalities in a given year, like 2013
  • difficult to compare gender ratio of a nationality with another, in a given year
  • color works great for indicating gender, but what about STEM vs BHASE? it would be too complicated to have 2 visual encodings for 2 categorical dimensions.

Process

I came up with the following solutions for the stated problems:

The right visual encodings that reduce the cognitive load

  • Bar chart where number of students by nationality and gender are mapped to y axis, because we are the most accurate at mapping length to a value in our brains according to Stevens power law.
  • Color to indicate the field of study (STEM vs BHASE), because it is best for distinguishing between discrete categories.

2 facets

  • 2 facets allowed me to use less visual encodings: 2 simple facets instead of one complex one. I did not have to use a new one for gender, instead I used length just like with nationality.
  • 2 facets created the oppurtunity for interactivity, which itself was another solution.

Interactivity

  • A slider that can be used to change the year, this way we can see how the number of students from different regions change over the years dynamically.
  • Dragging over a chart filtering the results on the other chart, this way we can see the gender ratio for different regions, in different years.
