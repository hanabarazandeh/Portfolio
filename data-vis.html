Overview

University enrolment is an Observable tool with a series of visualizations that I designed and developed to explore post secondary enrolment patterns in Canada.

The main goal of this project was to design a tool that makes a very large dataset into insight; more specifically, help the audience discover the relationship between different dimensions.

I will talk about a visualization in this project that I consider the most important:

International university enrolment by country of citizenship, gender, and field study, and year

I aimed to design a visualization that could find some patterns, and help find the right questions. Questions like:

  • If some nationalities among international students are increasing faster over the years, are these values proportional to the population of those regions?
  • If female students are increasing in STEM enrolments, is it the same rate among different nationalities?

And then I created other visualizations that try to answer those questions.

Challenge 1

Choosing the right visual encodings that reduce the cognitive load

Solution 1

  • Bar chart, because we are the most accurate at mapping length to a value in our brains according to Stevens power law.
  • Color to indicate the field of study (STEM vs BHASE), because it is best for distinguishing between discrete categories.

Challenge 2

Too many dimensions (4), difficult to observe all of these at once, with one visualization:

  • Number of students from different regions throughout the years.
  • Change in number of students from, for instance, Middle East throughout the years.
  • STEM vs BHASE ratio in different nationalities.
  • Gender ratio among different nationalities, throughout the years.

Solution 2

Interactivity:

  • A slider that can be used to change the year, this way we can see how the number of students from different regions change over the years dynamically.
  • Dragging over a chart filtering the results on the other chart, this way we can see the gender ratio for different regions, in different years.
